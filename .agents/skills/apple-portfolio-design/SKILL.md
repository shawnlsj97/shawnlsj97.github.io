# Apple Portfolio Design

Use this skill when designing, reviewing, or polishing Shawn's portfolio UI.

## Design Principles

- Treat the site as a product landing page for Shawn as a senior engineer, not a resume dump.
- Prefer restraint, whitespace, strong hierarchy, calm typography, and precise spacing.
- Make the experience feel premium, quiet, fast, and technically mature.
- Use subtle depth, thin borders, refined contrast, and premium-feeling interactions.
- Keep motion minimal and fast, and respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Prioritize recruiter scanning: identity, impact, experience, projects, skills, contact.

## Avoid

- Flashy student-portfolio aesthetics.
- Neon gradients, particle backgrounds, gimmicks, and novelty interactions.
- Excessive shadows, oversized cards, heavy animation, and decorative clutter.
- Dense resume-like paragraphs or equal visual weight for every historical item.
- Visuals that distract from Shawn's senior engineering positioning.

## Review Output Format

When reviewing UI, output a table with these columns:

| Area | Issue | Recommended improvement | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

Keep recommendations specific and implementation-ready.
