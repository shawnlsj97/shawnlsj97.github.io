# Senior Engineer Copy

Use this skill when writing or revising portfolio copy.

## Voice

- Concise, credible, technically strong, polished, and confident without hype.
- Senior engineer, not student or junior developer.
- Clear over clever.
- Specific over broad.
- Impact-oriented over task-list oriented.

## Emphasize

- Reliability, scale, ownership, maintainability, secure engineering, and platform thinking.
- Payments and financial systems experience where relevant.
- Full-stack engineering only where it strengthens the story.
- AI/ML, RAG, agentic systems, and developer productivity where supported by evidence.
- Engineering judgment: tradeoffs, constraints, collaboration, and measurable outcomes.

## Constraints

- Generalize confidential Visa work.
- Do not invent metrics.
- Flag places where Shawn should provide actual numbers.
- Avoid buzzword stuffing.
- Avoid overly casual language.
- Avoid long resume-style paragraphs.

## Preferred Patterns

- Use sharp summaries followed by impact-oriented bullets.
- Lead with scope and outcome.
- Keep project descriptions structured: problem, approach, impact, technologies.
- Use active verbs, but avoid inflated claims.
- Prefer "built", "designed", "optimized", "led", "improved", "reduced", "supported", and "shipped" when accurate.
